Though Line 3 Scarborough is not affected by the upcoming TTC cuts, it is bound to be closed in late 2023.

Line 3 Scarborough has been the backbone for transit in the east end for over 37 years, and despite the lack of rapid transit in the nearby area, it is set to close in less than a few months. Though the TTC is extending Line 2 Bloor-Danforth to Sheppard East, this extension will not be open until 2030. This will force 35,000 daily SRT riders on shuttle buses.

Over the past few years, TTCriders advocated and eventually succeeded in a rapid bus lane to diminish the affects of the closure of the SRT. This rapidway will also include much needed green space for walking and cycling.

However, we still need much more transit in Scarborough. Read our report and sign your name to help!

City Council's recent TTC budget proposes a 9% cut to TTC service.

Here's what that means...


Subway wait times could be up to
10 minutes in off peak hours, and it'll be crowded

Subway service will be 6 minutes or better at peak. Moreover, subway service is being cut 25% from pre-pandemic levels. Bus service will be cut 13% from pre-pandemic levels, and streetcars will be cut 6% from pre-pandemic levels.

You'll pay more for less

Adult PRESTO fares will increase to $3.30 whilst youth fares will increase to $2.35- 10 cents more than before. City Council is asking the lowest-income Toronto residents to pay more to get to school and work, rather than raising revenue from those who can afford it. This increase falls most heavily on shift workers, women, and low-income people who rely on transit the most.

These cuts will impact the people who rely on it the most

Raktim Mitra and Tess Peterman from Toronto Metropolitan University concluded that 26 of the 28 TTC routes that will signficantly be cut go through neighbourhoods with high material deprivation (an indicator of poverty concentration). A report by John Stapleton, Yvonne Yuan, and Richard Maaranen also concludes this.

Additionally, 24 of the 28 TTC routes that will signficantly be cut go through neighbourhoods with more visual minorities and immigrants.

Finally, 22 of the 28 TTC routes that will signficantly be cut connect with neighbourhoods in the north-east and west ends of the city- an area with already poor transit infrastructure. The closure of Line 3 Scarborough at the end of 2023 will simply make this worse.
